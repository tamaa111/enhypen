ENHYPEN is a South Korean boy band formed by BE:LIFT Lab, a joint venture between Hybe Corporation and CJ ENM, through a survival competition show, I-Land. The group consist of seven multinational members JUNGWON, HEESEUNG, JAY, JAKE, SUNGHOON, SUNOO, and NI-KI. They debuted on November 30th, 2020, and are known for their dynamic and highly synchronized performances, which have garnered them international praise as the “4th Generation Hot Icons” and “Global K-pop Rising Stars.” Their fandom name is ENGENE, which is derived from the words “engine” and “gene.”

LEADER

JUNGWON

PRE-DEBUT

  • Jungwon was a former SM trainee before he joined Big Hit. A mom of a friend who was also a Big Hit trainee at the time, showed Jungwon’s photo to a casting director, and that was how he got recruited.

  • When he was still in I-LAND, he put ‘acrobatics’ as his hidden talent.

  • Jungwon has a sister who is 2 years older than him and he said they have very similar eyes.

  • He was praised by Bang PD during ILAND for having a unique and charming voice.

  • Jungwon is described by his classmates as 100% pure milk, really kind, soft-spoken, and laughs a lot with his friends.

  • Jungwon was the class president for 5 years straight in his class followed by being the vice president in 6th grade.

  • He was selected to become ENHYPEN’s leader after a 40-day selection process, which included interviews with all members and tests.

CENTER

HEESUNG

PRE-DEBUT

  • Heeseung is from Uiwang, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea.

  • He has an older brother who is 2 years older than him.

  • He started dreaming to become a singer when he was 6.

  • He covered BTS’ DNA during a school function with his friends.

  • After taking an entrance test at Hanlim Arts High school, he was approached by 7-8 company officials to cast him.

  • Bighit Entertainment wanted the contract the soonest, and he wanted to be there as well, so he signed the contract.

  • He sang the song Woo-ah (우아해) by Crush for his audition at Bighit.

  • As a Bighit trainee, he was the trainee representative for the ‘New Staff Development Team’

  • He trained under BigHit for 3 years and 1 month before he transferred to BE:LIFT Lab.

  • During the BTS Test in I-Land, he received the highest score of 93 of the show for his Fake Love performance.

  • In the final episode of I-Land, he placed 5th by global votes with a total of 1,137,323 votes

Rapper

JAY

PRE-DEBUT

  • Jay was born in Seattle, Washington but moved to Korea when he was 9 years old.

  • Jay, along with Heeseung, Sunghoon and Jungwon were trainees under Big Hit Entertainment.

  • He trained for 2 years and 11 months under Big Hit Entertainment.

  • He attended LP Dance Academy prior to joining Big Hit Entertainment.

  • He trained for two years and eleven months before taking part in I-LAND.

  • He earned second place in the final of I-LAND (1,182,889 votes).

  • He and Sunghoon performed NCT U‘s The 7th Sense together in the first episode of I-LAND.

  • He is an only child. Both of his parents are Korean.

VOCALIST

JAKE

PRE-DEBUT

  • Jake was born in South Korea but moved to Australia when he was 9 years old.

  • He has a border collie named Layla, who was named after Eric Clapton’s song with the same name. Layla was born on October 31, 2014 and was previously their neighbor’s dog until Jake’s family adopted her.

  • In Australia, Jake was a Dwight School soccer team representative. His position was left wing forward.

  • Prior to moving to Korea, He played violin for four years and was in the school orchestra.

  • Jake also played bass at his church in Australia.

  • He sang Justin Bieber’s “Love Yourself’ for the Belift global audition in his country and was the only one selected out of 500 applicants.

  • Jake moved from Australia to Korea in June 2019. He trained for 9 months before entering the survival show I-land.

  • Jake was labeled as the “Icon of Growth” due to his outstanding growth in a short amount of time during I-land.

  • He finished 3rd place on I-land with 1,179,633 votes in the final episode.

SUNOO

PRE-DEBUT

  • Sunoo trained under Belift Lab for 10 months prior to participating in I-Land

  • During Be:Lift’s audition for I-Land, he beat 199 other contestants to be selected for the reality show.

  • With his love for singing and dancing, he actively participates in his middle school’s annual choir competition

  • Sunoo attended Chilbo Middle School, where he was a choir student. Sunoo participated in choir competition every year.

  • He also attended Chilbo High School until he formally joined ENHYPEN, after which he switched schools to Hanlim Multi Art School, where he graduated on February 11th, 2022.

  • Sunoo had a crab, snail, and guppie as pets growing up.

  • He learned hapkido and piano when he was young.

  • Sunoo ranked 1st multiple times during I-land by global votes.

  • Before debut, Sunoo was class president and a student council member at his school.

  • In the final episode of I-Land, Sunoo was the producer’s pick, having earned a total of 935,771 votes.

SUNGHOON

PRE-DEBUT

  • Sunghoon is from Namyangju, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea.

  • He has a sister who is five years younger.

  • He was a former competitive figure skater who competed both nationally and internationally for 10 years.

  • He passed all 8 Figure Skating Levels before retiring in early 2020.

  • He had severe amblyopia as a child, making it difficult for him to see with his right eye.

  • Sunghoon started out with playing Ice Hockey to improve his social skills but turned to figure skating when he was nine years old, after his parents recommended it to him.

  • In 2014 he appeared on the SBS program “Crayon in my Mind“, where producers followed him around for a week to capture the daily life of a young male figure skater.

  • He was scouted by BigHit in 2018 and trained there for 2 years and 1 month before entering the survival show program I-Land.

  • Sunghoon was casted through Instagram DMs.

  • Initially, Sunghoon had no intentions of being an idol and trained only to improve his dance and figure skating skills, but he later fell in love with it and decided to change careers.

  • Sunghoon did Ballet when he was 10 years old.

  • He has a French poodle named Gaeul, which translates to Autumn in English.

DANCER

NI-KI

PRE-DEBUT

  • He was born in Okayama Prefecture, Japan.

  • He started dancing at the age of 3. He was initially self-taught, starting with jazz and imitating Michael Jackson’s dances.

  • He used to imitate Michael Jackson for 5 hours a day when he was young.

  • At age 5, he joined a dance academy where he first learned ballet.

  • Prior to traveling to Korea to become a trainee, Ni-ki hosted his own workshops at LEAD Entertainment, a dance studio owned by Ni-ki’s family.

  • He came to South Korea on August 1st, 2019 when he was 13 (international age) by himself. From then until his participation in I-Land he lived alone in Seoul as a trainee at a home-stay.

  • He trained under BE:LIFT for 8 months and was a famous trainee for his exceptional dance skills.

  • Ni-ki was ranked 4th in the final episode of I-LAND with 1,140,718 votes.

  • He made another official cover video of ‘Lie’ that he posted on ENHYPEN official account in 2020 when he was 15.

  • Ni-ki has studied various genres of dance, including jazz, ballet, hip hop, b-boying, contemporary, latino, kpop, and freestyle. Ni-ki can also do acrobatics.

  • Ni-ki performed at the Pan Pacific Festival in Oahu, Hawaii in 2016 where he danced to a medley of Michael Jackson songs.
